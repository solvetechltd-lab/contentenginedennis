Section 01 · Overview

How the system works.

One repeatable weekly engine. Seven posts a week, each with a fixed role and a deliberate call-to-action. The structure never changes — that's what makes it sustainable to run and easy to batch. What rotates is the angle, so the audience never sees the same pitch twice in a row.

The usual way Post when inspiration strikes. Random topics, inconsistent cadence, no clear ask — so nothing converts.
→
The McQ system A fixed weekly rhythm with two dedicated "money posts" and a planned ask every single day. Predictable input, compounding output.
What you get A fully mapped 8-week calendar (56 posts), 16 founder-video topics queued and ready to script, and a repurposing playbook that turns each video into four platform-native posts.

Section 04 · Conversion Logic

CTA discipline.

Built on Brunson value-ladder logic: each post type has exactly one job. We don't ask for the call every day — we earn the right to ask, then ask deliberately twice a week.

1
Book a Call — 2×/week

On the two video days. The core conversion ask.

2
Lead Magnet — 1×/week (Fri)

Captures emails into GoHighLevel.

3
Blog / Website — 2×/week (Wed, Sat)

Nurtures the audience and drives traffic.

4
Engagement — 1×/week (Sun)

Earns reach with no ask.

The key idea Two dedicated "money posts" a week, every week. The other five posts make those two land — by the time we ask for the call, the audience already trusts the brand.
Section 05 · Leverage

One script, four posts.

Each founder video is recorded once and adapted into four platform-native posts — maximum reach from minimum extra effort.

Platform
LinkedIn

Native upload + a 3–5 line written hook above it. Professional tone.

Platform
Facebook

Same video, slightly warmer caption. Tag the McQ page.

Platform
Instagram

Reel, vertical 9:16, punchy on-screen captions, 3–5 hashtags.

Platform
YouTube Short

Vertical <60s cut, keyword title, link to website in description.

Production tip Batch-record videos — shoot 4 at once (two weeks of content) to stay ahead and protect Dennis's time.
